Stuttering Disorder

Stuttering is a speech disorder that disrupts the natural flow of speech. It involves repeated sounds, syllables, or words, as well as prolongations or blocks where speech is momentarily stopped. People who stutter may also exhibit physical signs like rapid blinking or tension in the face. It can impact communication, social interactions, and self-esteem.

Specialist Of Therapist: Speech-Language Pathologists (SLPs)

Voice Disorder

Abnormal pitch, loudness, or voice quality impacting speech. Causes include vocal cord issues, strain, or medical conditions. It can cause discomfort or affect communication.

Specialist Of Therapist: Otolaryngologists (ENT Specialists).

Read More

Neurological Speech Issue

Speech problems due to nervous system disorders, like dysarthria (muscle control issues) or aphasia (language processing issues). It can affect speech clarity and communication.

Specialist of Therapist: Neurologists.

pediatric Speech Delay

Delay in speech and language development in children, impacting communication and learning. It may result from hearing issues, developmental delays, or other underlying conditions.

Specialists of Therapist: Pediatricians, Developmental Pediatricians.

Autism Communication Issue

Challenges in social communication, including delayed speech, repetitive language, and difficulty understanding non-verbal cues. It impacts social interactions and expressive language.
